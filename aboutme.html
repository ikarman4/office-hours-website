Professional

I am a graduating junior at Boston University concentrating in computational neuroscience with a minor in computer science. Currently, I am conducting research under the supervision of BUâ€™s Computational Neuroscience and Vision lab in order to bridge the gap between our neural circuitry and the perceptual world around us. Whether it be the lines and lines of game development, the scripts for data visualization, or just messing around in virtual reality, the mixture of real neural modeling and systems with software and mechanics brings out the science fiction fan in me, encouraging my pursuits of grasping how we come to understand the world around us. With these lessons in mind, I began a project to connect undergraduate students in neuroscience to industry leaders to explore different definitions of what it means to be a neuroscientist. Using a podcast as a comfortable medium of communication, my co-host and I began to peer into what it means to study the brain and how that incorporates itself into all aspects of life -even talking to an engineer of the international space station! Our podcast, Office Hours, allowed me to network with some of the foremost neuroscientists, taught me how to navigate a professional setting and adapt to each unseen circumstances whether it be losing three thousand dollars in start-up funding due to Covid-19 or rebuilding the front end of our website from basic HTML to React and Node.js.

Personal

I am ambitious to jump into the industry of biotechnologies and explore my professional opportunities, however, I have to keep myself busy in the meantime. I love exploring the city of Boston through great restaurants, vintage stores, and art galleries. Currently I carry my Sony A7iii for quick bursts of photographic creativity throughout the city. When I'm not out and about I am usually reading sci-fi novels, playing in virtual reality, and baking chocolate chip banana bread.
